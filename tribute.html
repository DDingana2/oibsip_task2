Mahatma Gandhi


1869 - 1948

Biography

  • Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, born on October 2,1869 ,was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule.


  • Gandhi is recognized as the Father of the Nation in India and is known for his principles of truth, nonviolence, and peaceful protest. He led several nonviolent civil disobedience campaigns, including the Salt March and Quit India Movement, which inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

  • Gandhi's philosophy of Satyagraha, meaning "truth force" or "soul force," remains influential to this day, and his teachings on nonviolence, communal harmony, and self-discipline continue to inspire people around the globe.
